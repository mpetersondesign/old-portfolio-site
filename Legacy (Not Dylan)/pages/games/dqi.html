Dragon Quest I -- Story

As a descent of a legendary hero, you must protect and defend the land of Alefgard against hordes of monsters that suddenly appeared with the rise of the "Dragonlord", a mysterious fiend who's teamed up with the forces of monsters and dragons to take over Alefgard. You must save the princess, find the Goddess' secret artifacts, retrace your ancestor, the legendary hero, and follow in his footsteps to save the world.

Dragon Quest I -- History

Dragon Quest ("Dragon Warrior in the US") was the first game released in the Dragon Quest series. First released in 1986 for the Famicom in Japan, Dragon Quest sold over 2 million copies. It released as Dragon Warrior in North America 3 years later and did considerably worse with a western audience, thus the series' fallthrough with American audiences.


Dragon Quest I focuses on the most basic of RPG elements, creating tropes and setting trends for decades of JRPGs to come, such as a large overworld map in the style of the game's world, random encounters, enemy sprites, battle backgrounds, and open-world exploration and dungeon crawling. All of these new features became staples for the series, and later became better known in the west through Dragon Quest's lesser successor, Final Fantasy.

Dragon Quest I -- Gameplay

The first installation in the series, Dragon Quest I focuses on a solo adventure through an open world, with only one party member the game forces you to navigate and defeat enemies completely based on a single character's attributes. For this reason, many people attribute the first game to be either the series' easiest or hardest game in the series depending on the player's party preferences. The game also focuses on figuring out obscure hints and puzzles in varied locations in order to obtain key items to progress the story.
