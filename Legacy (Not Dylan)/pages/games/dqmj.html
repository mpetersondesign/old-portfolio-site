Dragon Quest Monsters: Joker

You wake up in a jail cell as one of your father's guards bangs on the bars, demanding your presence. Your father ridicules your escape attempt to go join the "Monster Scout Challenge" but then concedes as he has a mission for you that runs parallel. He sets you free, and you join the ranks of the Monster Scouts on the Domus isles. Recruit legendary monsters, figure out the secret of Incarnus, discover the mission your father laid out for you, and of course, save the world.

Dragon Quest Monsters: Joker is the best game in the series. It is also the best Pokemon game. It does everything right and nothing wrong. Dragon Quest Monsters combines everything that's "good" about Pokemon and Final Fantasy and does it better. Explore varied and colorful locations, with new monsters at every turn to scout into your party. Humans take a back seat to Akiya Toriyama's varied and unique monster selection in this game. You fight using monsters, you breed using monsters, you breathe monsters. Have too many monsters? Combine your monsters to make a new better monster, then combine that one with a combination you were already working with to make another new stronger monster. Not enough? Combine that monster with that new monster you just scouted on that endgame isle and make an even BETTER monster. You want a final boss from any of the previous games? Make it.


Dragon Quest Monsters was such a popular spinoff, it got a sequel, and then that spinoff got spinoffs. Dragon Quest Monsters for the GBC, Dragon Quest Monsters II for the GBC, finally Dragon Quest Monsters I + II in North America, then their spinoffs, Joker and Joker 2, and they even got extra versions in Japan and a whole new installment with Joker 3 (you can play it with an english patch and an emulator if you know where to find it) and new 3D remakes of the first two. Dragon Quest Monsters is the most popular spinoff Dragon Quest media.


Joker 1 and 2 are both equally good, both offering a new breath on the same formula with small changes and a larger monster selection and postgame in the sequel. Joker 2 is an amazing collectathon with many types of monsters appearing at every corner of the game. It also has one of the best post-game missions I've seen in a JRPG.
