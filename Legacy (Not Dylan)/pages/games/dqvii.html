Dragon Quest VII -- Story

Follow the three main heroes, a child from the port town of Pilchard, his best friend Maribel, and a wandering Prince Keifer to uncover the mystery behind the magical fragments popping up all over the island of Estard, seemingly leading to other islands off the coast lost in the past. Assemble pieces of the world map as you piece together history and the lives of the people who lived on the vanished islands.

Dragon Quest VII -- History

Dragon Quest VII, released in 2000 for the Playstation and remade for the 3DS in 2013 was the first game since Dragon Warrior IV to be released in North America on launch (whereas DQV and DQVI would later be re-released many years after their launch for the first times in North America on the DS) and was well-praised in the west. The game is by far the longest in the series, with over 100+ hours for the main story.


Dragon Quest VII on the Nintendo 3DS was extremely well-received by fans of the series. It was pleasantly modern and updated, offering fully 3D rendered graphics true to the spirit of the original game, something not a lot of handheld JRPGs offered at the time, especially not Final Fantasy.

Dragon Quest VII -- Gameplay

Dragon Quest VII's gameplay is very similar to that of Dragon Quest VI's, following in the sacred game design footsteps of the vocation system. However, something that is true to DQVII that sets it apart from the series is it's lack of emphasis on an open world, instead favoring for a linear path to "unlock" each area of the game. This plays surprisingly well, as it makes each area of the world different and unique and a mystery before relic collection.
