Dragon Quest IV -- Story

The secret of evolution; a hidden technique that can turn humans into monsters, and make monsters more monstrous, is sought after by an evil wandering swordsman, Psaro, to extract revenge on all humans. Propechy tells of a Legendary Hero who will inhabit the equipment of the heavens, traverse through the sky under the holy watch of the Zenith Dragon, and put an end to evils' reign. Choose between either Solo or Sofia and explore the backstories of all your party members before heading off together in a wagon trip to save the world from Psaro and the secret of evolution.

Dragon Quest IV -- History

Dragon Quest IV ("Dragon Warrior 4 in the US") released in 1990, though it's most notable appearance was on the PSX in 2001, skipping the SNES remake stage entirely, unlike the previous games. At the time, Dragon Quest IV served as the black sheep of the series, being the first game not to focus on the lineage of Erdrick, instead starting a new trilogy in the series dubbed the "Zenithian Trilogy" by fans, as it focuses on the legend of Zenithia, a floating castle in the sky, and the Zenith Dragon, a godlike being that looks over the realm of the heavens.


Dragon Quest IV saw various releases, on the NES, the PSX, and the DS later on, with iOS and Android remakes coming afterwards as apart of Square-Enix's plan to bring every Dragon Quest as a portable game to the west. Surprisingly, Dragon Quest IV did very well in the west, winning "Best Game Grand Prize", "Best RPG", and "Best-Seller" in Famitsu, and winning multiple awards in Nintendo Power '93. The game was even popular enough in Japan to start the trend of spinoff games based on individual party characters and their backstories.

Dragon Quest IV -- Gameplay

Differing from its predecessors, Dragon Quest IV does let you choose from various party members, but much unlike Dragon Quest III, these characters are pre-made and have fleshed out backstories you must explore prior to playing with them in the main chapter. The game is divided up into 5/6 chapters, depending on which versions of the game you play. The first 4 being introductory chapters to each party member's story, and the 5th being the "main" game where you have the option of choosing from all of the characters.
