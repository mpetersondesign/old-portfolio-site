Dragon Quest VI -- Story

Follow the Zenithian hero as he confronts the evil Murdaw, only to fall in battle and be sent to a mysterious realm where it is impossible to tell dream from reality. Forced to re-connect with his party and meet new friends to get back to his reality, and find his own body, the hero and company will discover a bigger evil is at play.

Dragon Quest VI -- History

Dragon Quest VI was considered the black sheep of the series upon its release in 1995. It's bizarre plot, theming, structure, and dialogue left much to be desired of from its Japanese audience. It was surprisingly well-received by it's western audience, however, when it was re-released on the Nintendo DS. Despite it's somewhat negative reception, it is highly praised for improving on what Dragon Quest V lacked; graphic and sound design. Despite the love that people had for V, people greatly appreciated the improvement on DQVI as it set itself apart more from its 8-bit counterparts.


Dragon Quest VI was Japan's "Best-Selling Game" of 1995, each copy of Dragon Quest VI being over 100 USD in Japan, it sold 3.2 million copies. Dragon Quest VI was going to be localized as "Dragon Quest V" in North America after its Japanese release, but Enix thought that the game would be too complicated of a sell to western audiences, and that Final Fantasy, a much simpler and archaic series, would prove to be more successful in North America.

Dragon Quest VI -- Gameplay

Dragon Quest VI makes a much needed improvement to it's predecessor's gameplay; Vocations. Vocations are class-based systems that your character can inherit to influence stat and spell growth and availability. Each Vocation (such as mage, warrior, priest, gadabout, etc.) can be used to promote your character to a second-tier vocation, such as a Gladiator (Martial artist + warrior), Paladin (Priest + Warrior), Sage (Priest + Mage) etc. This system was later implemented into the Dragon Quest III remake, making that game the best in the early series.
