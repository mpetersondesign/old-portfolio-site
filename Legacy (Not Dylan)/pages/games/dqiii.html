Dragon Quest III -- Story

Before the events of the original Dragon Quest, Dragon Quest III gives you control of the legendary hero, Erdrick, as they follow in their father's footsteps to try and defeat the evil Baramos, a sorceror trying to take over the hero's homeland of Aliahan, however, something more sinister takes the hero all the way back to Alefgard.

Dragon Quest III -- History

Dragon Quest III ("Dragon Warrior 3 in the US") released in 1988. It was the most highly praised Dragon Quest at the time, taking a ton of innovation to implement the most new features of any title in the series. Dragon Quest III sold over one million copies on it's first day, causing almost 300 arrests for truancy among students who skipped school to purchase the game. This caused Enix, the publishers, to make a rule to appease the Japanese government to not release any more Dragon Quest games on school days or work days, as everyone would be too busy playing to do their work.


The game sold tremendously well, earning it's spot in 2006's list of "greatest JRPGs of all time", falling short only of inferior Final Fantasy titles due to the generational gap in gaming technology. The game was a commercial failure, however, in America, due to the fact the game wasn't brought over until later in gaming's history, making the game seem archaic to western audiences.

Dragon Quest III -- Gameplay

Dragon Quest III is the most unique and innovative game in the series to date. Offering many new features such as a class and job system, new types of weapons and magic, customizable party members, stat changes based on gender and personality, (where a "sexy" female will always have higher stats then the best personality for a male character with few exceptions, no less.) male and female hero options, new methods of transportation, a MUCH bigger world map, endgame content, and new battle mechanics based on the 4-party system. Dragon Quest III set the precedent for almost every single RPG that came after, and would be one of the most influential RPGs in video game history.
