Dragon Quest VIII -- Story

When an evil being steals a magic scepter from the kingdom of Trode, it's up to the Hero to save his friends from the curse of Dhoulmagus and set out with new friends, Yangus, Jessica, and Angelo to defeat Dhoulmagus. However, it might be something out of their field of view that is really at play..

Dragon Quest VIII -- History

Dragon Quest VIII was the first Dragon Quest released in North America, finally dropping the "Warrior" moniker. (However, Dragon Quest V is "technically" the "earliest" Dragon Warrior to lose the 'warrior' title) It was many player's introduction to the series in the west, and commercially successful in America compared to its predecessors. It boasted over-the-shoulder 3D graphics, impressive effects, catchy music, and sold well to the new generation of 3D JRPG players. Included with the game was a demo of an inferior game, Final Fantasy XII.


Dragon Quest VIII is praised for being the first Dragon Quest to include fully voiced lines in the series, occuring where story is present. All of the traditional 8-bit sounding SFX were also changed in this game and modernized for the series onward. Many previous Dragon Quest cameos are also present in this game, such as characters from I, III, and IV.

Dragon Quest VIII -- Gameplay

For the first time (excluding Dragon Quest V and Dragon Quest VII's 3D remakes) a Dragon Quest game is in stunning 3D graphics. The over-the-shoulder 3D view was insanely popular for its time and lead to a lot of interesting game mechanics, and changed the way that you explore and view the world and villages. Lots of gameplay changes were made in the 3DS remake specifically, such as following Dragon Quest IX's trend of substituting random encounters in favor of 3D models of the monsters roaming in the overworld. Dragon Quest VIII also offers a much-needed "speedup" feature to battles, think Awakening or Fates.
