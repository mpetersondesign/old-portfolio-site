Dragon Quest II -- Story

100 years after the Dragonlord attempted to take over Alefgard, the offspring of the hero who defeated him find themselves on the continent of Torland, consisting of three kingdoms; Middenhall, Cannock, and Moonbrooke. Take control of the Prince of Middenhall as you gather your companions, the Prince of Cannock and the Princess of Moonbrooke to take down a new evil-- the evil sorceror Hargon, who plans to resurrect an even greater evil on the world. Together, you must re-discover Alefgard, coordinate your party in combat, and uncover the secrets of your ancestors and the Goddess.

Dragon Quest II -- History

Dragon Quest II ("Dragon Warrior 2 in the US") released in 1987, a year after its predecessor, and was very positively received for its additions to the series. Dragon Quest II was the first Dragon Quest game to feature multiple party members, a mechanic that was greatly improved upon in later titles. It also offered new methods of traversing the main overworld, and adding mechanics to dungeons and towns.


Much of the game's dialogue and even some animation sequences had to be removed due to strict censorship in America. Although the game was widely praised for its improvements on the series in Japan, the game once again fell flat on western audiences who were more attuned to fast-paced gameplay offered by other titles on the NES. However, the game was popular enough in Japan to spawn one of the series first alternative media, a novelization based on "Dragon Quest II".

Dragon Quest II -- Gameplay

Dragon Quest II offered many improvements on the gameplay of the first game. The most prominent being a multi-unit party. Being able to control up to three heroes in combat at once added a new dimension to JRPG battles, being able to add more enemies and harder bosses to compensate for the extra firepower. New spells and combat mechanics were also added to build on this concept. The overworld map is also now twice as big as it was in Dragon Quest I, a miniature version of Alefgard even residing in the north-western part of the world map.
