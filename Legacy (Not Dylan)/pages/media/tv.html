TV Anime -- Dragon Quest

Dragon Quest was an anime released in 1989 based on the game of the same name. Thirteen episodes made it to the west, dubbed by Saban. The plot follows the hero, Abel, and his best friend, Tiala. The evil dragon Baramos captures Tiala, and Abel must set out to defeat him to rescue her. Abel meets many companions in his 43-episode adventure, and they all venture together to save Tiala and defeat Baramos.


TV Anime -- Dragon Quest Dai's Great Adventure

Dragon Quest - Dai no Daibōken was an extremely popular Shonen Jump! series that ran and was adapted into a TV anime. Dai's Great Adventure is generally accepted as the definitive Dragon Quest anime, and the series was popular enough to even feature Dai himself in the 2019 anime fighting game "Jump Force!". Dai's Great Adventure follows the young hero Dai as he teams up with monsters and friends alike to take down the evil that threatens his island, featuring many characters and references from previous games.


Television -- Yūsha Yoshihiko to Maō no Shiro

Yūsha Yoshihiko to Maō no Shiro, while not explicitly "Dragon Quest", is considered to be the first best piece of Dragon Quest-related media on live television. It's a parody drama of popular JRPG tropes, however it's main character and most of the monsters are DIRECTLY referenced from Dragon Quest V and Dragon Quest games respectively. It is extremely funny, offers a lot of comedic insight into Dragon Quest's various tropes, and ran a pretty recent release, so it's not hard to find.


Film -- Dragon Quest: Your Story

Your Story, sadly, was Dragon Quest's answer to 3D CG feature films. It's visually dazzling, the voices are all well-done, the effects are amazing, and it has the potential to be an amazing movie. Sadly, they made it an isekai. However, Dragon Quest: Your Story, while yes, having the worst reviews of any movie this year, is the one feature-length Dragon Quest film.
