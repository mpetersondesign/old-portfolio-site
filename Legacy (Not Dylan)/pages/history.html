History of Dragon Quest

Dragon Quest was created by Yuji Horii, alongside Akira Toriyama, based on the inspirations of Ultima and Wizardry. Dragon Quest took the top-down movement system from Ultima and combined it with the first-person random battles from Wizardry and created the base for all future JRPGS to come. It captivated all of Japan and directly inspired most RPGs to come after. It was simple, it was plain, but it was fun and it was enough to spawn an entire phenomena. Dragon Quest owes much of its lineage to Dragon Ball series creator Akira Toriyama, who's monster designs took Japan by storm when Dragon Quest hit shelves. Dragon Quest has NEVER been favored by western audiences, most North Americans preferring the vastly inferior Final Fantasy, and despite this Dragon Quest has managed to spawn many games in the US, and even a more recent appearance in Super Smash Brothers Ultimate, being one of the most vastly developed additions to the game, one of the biggest home run hitters for western fans of the game. Dragon Quest still sees a rise in popularity today with XI: S coming to Switch this year.


Cultural Influence

Dragon Quest has had an exceptionally profound impact on Japanese culture. The original is generally accreditted to be the first home-console RPG. GameSpot has even cited Dragon Quest as one of the most influential games of all time, with it being THE foremost most influential RPG of all time, saying "Nearly every single JRPG since then have drawn from Dragon Quest's gameplay in some way". Dragon Quest is extremely topical in Japan, there have been many news events, parades, and events to celebrate Dragon Quest games, and it considered by MANY Japanese citizens to be their country's "national game". It is common knowledge that if you ask anyone in Japan to draw the word "slime", they will draw the weakest mob from Dragon Quest, the slime.
